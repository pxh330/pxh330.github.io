Something about me

Hey it’s me, Xiaohan! (If you find Xiaohan a bit hard to pronounce you can call me Sharon:)) I’m Chinese, 21 yrs old, doing Electrical and Electronic Engineering undergrad at University of Nottingham in UK. Currently I’m doing my industrial placement in IMS&IN Team at Vodafone UK headquarters. Just in case you’ve never heard of ‘industrial placement’, so in UK industrial placement is quite like a gap year for internship, but it’s a part of your degree and you usually do it after your second year if you are doing bachelor. Sounds cool right? YES I can tell you it is but I have to work from home these days because of coronavirus. (I’ve still got a lot of annual leave… I wish I travelled more last year☹.)

In my uni, studying EEE can be fun, especially when you are doing some geeky stuffs and your business school friends are like ‘wow this is so cool! Sharon’s so smart’ Lol! However, it is absolutely torture when you try hard to finish your project but you just…can’t… Weird things just happen all the time!

I'm never gonna be an electronic expert in the future because I’m never the best in my course. Well... This might sound like a stupid reason, but this was frustrating to me because I got so used to being the best easily in my high school years. Never the best is frustrating, but it's not too bad. I guess I might be the kind of person with loads of ‘useless’ knowledge in my head rather than a dedicated kind. I used to worry a lot about this, as I have many friends committing themselves in scientific research and they are doing amazing job. But my manager at Vodafone, Michael told me ‘It’s okay not being a dedicated kind'. He said 'the world needs people who focus on their area, but people with knowledge in different areas are also essential to the world, even if they are not the best.' His words were impressive and this was probably the very first time I no longer doubted myself. So, apart from my courses, I love coding, doing design and researching about food…… Frankly speaking, gradually I realize I’m quite a geeky but artistic girl, but these two sides surprisingly stay in harmony. Now, the 'glorious' scientific research will not make me jealous anymore, because I know I love what I am, and I'm wonderful cause I am.

However, so far I’m still a person who doesn’t make much sense to the world...... Hopefully I will in the future:)!

Thank you so much for reading all of these and listening to a very personal story from me. Now let's relax, how about exploring some
